Why a patio downtown?

Toronto is a 2026 host city, and even with no match in town on July 11, the city plays like it's in the tournament — flags on the patios, chants spilling onto the sidewalk, and strangers celebrating goals together. Mid-July means long evenings and warm nights, so an outdoor screen beats a dark sports bar.

The two quarter-finals bookend the day perfectly: catch Norway–England in the late-afternoon sun, order dinner between games, then settle in for Argentina–Switzerland under the lights. The core is walkable and transit-friendly (Union Station, King and Queen streetcars), so you can bar-hop if the mood shifts.

Everything below sits in or near the downtown core — the King West strip, the Entertainment District, Harbourfront, the Financial District and St. Lawrence. Tap any name to open its map, directions and official page.

Where to watch 26 patios & rooftops

A screen and a patio at every one. On a tournament Saturday these fill up fast — book ahead where you can, and show up early for the walk-in and RSVP spots.

Real Sports Bar & Grill

Maple Leaf Square · 15 York St

Toronto's flagship sports bar next to Scotiabank Arena, home to a 39-foot screen and a loud, packed matchday crowd. There's a front patio for the pre-game sun.

Reserve: Book online via OpenTable or realsports.ca — essential for big kick-offs.

RendezViews

Entertainment District · 229 Richmond St W

A former parking lot turned massive open-air fan zone — picnic tables, a giant screen, beach-volleyball and a CN Tower view. The city's best pure-patio watch party.

Reserve: Free RSVP on the RendezViews site (19+). First-come seating, so arrive early.

Café Diplomatico

Little Italy · 594 College St

A 60-year soccer institution. When a tournament is on, the big corner patio fills with fans of every nation and erupts after every goal. Italian comfort food and espresso.

Reserve: Walk-in / call ahead. Patio tables go early — come before kick-off.

Score on King

St. Lawrence · 107 King St E

Built for game day: TVs on every wall, a street-side patio, and a menu of burgers, wings and outsized Caesars. Easygoing and central.

Reserve: Walk-in / call ahead; larger groups can phone to hold a table.

Amsterdam Brewhouse

Harbourfront · 245 Queens Quay W

A sprawling lakefront brewery patio with screens, house-brewed beer and views across Lake Ontario to the CN Tower. Breezy spot for the afternoon match.

Reserve: Book on OpenTable — the patio is huge but sells out on sunny weekends.

Kōst

Bisha Hotel · 80 Blue Jays Way

A 44th-floor rooftop with an open-air terrace and skyline-and-lake views. A dressed-up Baja-California vibe for a special-occasion watch.

Reserve: Book on OpenTable well ahead; rooftop tables are limited.

Aera

The Well · 486 Front St W, 38th floor

One of the highest patios in the city, with sweeping views and an elevated menu. Their Wednesday "Open Aera" rooftop sessions are legendary — worth the ear-pop of the elevator.

Reserve: Book on OpenTable; ask specifically for the outdoor terrace.

Baro Rooftop

King West · 485 King St W

A Latin-infused fourth-floor rooftop that's enclosed and heated, so weather never cancels the plan. Cocktails, ceviche and a lively room.

Reserve: Book on OpenTable and request Baro Rooftop.

Lavelle

King West · 627 King St W

A rooftop pool-deck oasis with skyline views and a polished crowd. More see-and-be-seen than sports bar, but a memorable place to catch a goal.

Reserve: Book via the Lavelle website (rooftop tables + daybeds); reservations strongly recommended.

Earls King West

King West · 601 King St W

Three patios including a rooftop solarium overlooking the King strip. Reliable food, big drinks and plenty of screens — an easy crowd-pleaser.

Reserve: Book on OpenTable; note "rooftop" in the reservation request.

Cactus Club Cafe

Financial District · 77 Adelaide St W

A sleek patio tucked into First Canadian Place, right in the heart of the core. Polished service, upscale pub food and a buzzy after-work energy.

Reserve: Book on OpenTable — a solid Financial District option near Union.

The Rec Room

Roundhouse · 255 Bremner Blvd

Arcade games, an enormous screen and a covered patio steps from the CN Tower and Rogers Centre. Great for a group that wants more than just the match.

Reserve: Book tables via therecroom.com; big-screen showings can be reserved for groups.

The Loose Moose

Downtown · 146 Front St W

A go-to sports bar a two-minute walk from Union Station, with a big front patio and screens angled everywhere. Convenient for out-of-towners.

Reserve: Walk-in / call ahead; phone to hold a patio table for a group.

Wheat Sheaf Tavern

King West · 667 King St W

Toronto's oldest tavern (since 1849) with a sprawling covered patio and screens throughout. Unpretentious, boozy and perfect for a long double-header.

Reserve: Walk-in; call ahead for larger groups.

The Craft Brasserie & Grille

King West · 719 King St W

One of the biggest patios on King, backed by a wall of TVs and 100+ beers on tap. Loud, fun and made for a raucous matchday.

Reserve: Walk-in; call ahead for patio groups on game day.

Belfast Love Public House

King West · 548 King St W

Irish-pub warmth with a patio out front and a footie-loving crowd. Guinness, hearty plates and singalongs when the goals go in.

Reserve: Book via the Belfast Love website or walk in early.

WVRST

King West · 609 King St W

A German-style beer hall with communal tables, house sausages and a long list of drafts. There's a small patio, and the shared-table setup means instant matchday friends.

Reserve: Walk-in only — arrive before kick-off to grab bench space.

Bar Hop

King West · 391 King St W

A craft-beer favourite with a rooftop patio and dozens of rotating taps. More about great beer than wall-to-wall TVs, but they'll have the big games on.

Reserve: Walk-in; the rooftop is first-come, so head up early.

Northern Maverick Brewing Co.

King West · 115 Bathurst St

A spacious brewpub with a big patio, fresh house beer and screens for the match. Roomy enough that you can usually find space even on a busy day.

Reserve: Book on OpenTable or northernmaverick.ca.

Grace O'Malley's Irish Pub

Entertainment District · 14 Duncan St

A massive multi-level Irish pub with a rooftop patio and screens on every floor. Classic pints-and-football atmosphere in the middle of the district.

Reserve: Book via the website or walk in; ask about the rooftop for the evening game.

Hemingway's

Yorkville · 142 Cumberland St

A legendary multi-level rooftop patio complex just north of the core. Leafy, sprawling and always buzzing — a Toronto rite of passage on a warm night.

Reserve: Walk-in only, so arrive early — it packs out on sunny evenings.

Shoeless Joe's

Downtown · Adelaide St W

A dependable sports-bar chain done right downtown: dozens of screens, a patio, and a deep menu of wings and shareables. Nothing fancy, everything you need.

Reserve: Book or call via shoelessjoes.ca; walk-ins welcome.

Duke's Refresher & Bar

Downtown Yonge · 382 Yonge St

A big, screen-heavy downtown bar with a patio right on Yonge. Central, lively and easy to reach from anywhere on the subway.

Reserve: Walk-in / call ahead for groups.

P.J. O'Brien Irish Pub

St. Lawrence · 39 Colborne St

A handsome, old-school Irish pub on a quiet laneway with a tucked-away patio. Great pints and a proper football-pub feel a block from the King streetcar.

Reserve: Book via the website or call; the patio is small, so reserve ahead.

Track & Field Bar

Little Italy · 860 College St

A playful sports-and-games bar (bocce, skee-ball) with a back patio and a young, buzzy crowd. A fun alternative if you want a game between the games.

Reserve: Walk-in; call ahead to book a bocce court for a group.

Locals Public Eatery

Liberty Village · 171 E Liberty St

A neighbourhood sports-forward eatery with a big patio just west of the core. Screens, cold pitchers and an unfussy crowd — an easy call if downtown is packed.

Reserve: Book via the Locals website; walk-ins welcome.
